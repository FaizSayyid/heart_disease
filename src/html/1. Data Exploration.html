8. Agglomerating levels¶

Given we have so little data it might make sense to agglomerate categories that are similar in terms of heart disease risk. This will mean fewer categorical variables in a GLM or fewer splits in a tree based model. There will be very little data per level variable or per split. In addition categorical variables with more than two levels easily get lost inside tree based methods.¶

Before we agglomerate we should have a look at the variances in each level to see if it's plausable to perform the agglomeratiion¶

Agglomeration candidates are based upon that which we discovered above, specifically we'll look at:¶

In [59]:
#1. cp
#2. rest_ecg
#3. slope
#4. ca
#5. thal

When we model we'll produce three models:¶

1. Throw everything at it and let it "learn" the correct features, disposing of poor quality features during training¶

2. A second in which we supply hand tuned features.¶

3. A third in which we cut down the features based upon our eyeballing of the data¶

We'll produce three datasets for these three models. We'll call the modifications we've made so far the "kitchen_sink" dataset/model , the "hand_tuned" dataset/model and hand_tuned_feature_selected dataset/model¶
